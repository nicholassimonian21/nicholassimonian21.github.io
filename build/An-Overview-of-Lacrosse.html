An Overview of Lacrosse

Set-Up and Basic Strategies to Become a Strong Player

Nicholas Simonian

11/4/20 at 6:40pm

Lacrosse is a fast-paced game, and is one of the most action-packed sports on two feet. It is a team vs. team style game where the main objective is to score a goal on the opposing team's net. Each team has three atackers, midfielders, and defenders, and one goalie, in addition to a number of players on the bench who are ready to step in. Unlike soccer, substitutions occur fairly frequently in lacrosse due to its high-intensity nature. The game starts with a face-off between two plaers and ends after four quarters.

To get started, it is important to have the necessary equipment to remain safe. The equipment that is needed is a helmet, a pair of gloves, a pair of cleats, chest and shoulder pads, and a pair of elbow pads. These are intended to keep everyone safe because lacrosse is a contact sport, with emphasis on contact. It isn't as much as football, but lacrosse has its fair share of hits coupled with checks from the metal sticks. Now, in order to play the game, you should have at least one lacrosse stick and a couple lacrosse balls.

There are many simple exercises you can do to improve your skills in the game of lacrosse. The most important thing to do is to learn how to cradle, which is a way to keep the ball in your stick when you are running and geting checked by other players. Cradling requires a simple wrist-flick-like motion with the top hand. The game is also about teamwork, so working on your speed and catching and throwing abilitis are important as well. Wall ball, which is where you thorw a ball against a wall repeatedly, is an easy and efective method to practice that doesn't require multiple people.
